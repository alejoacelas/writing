# Call transcription with Granola

*Source: https://wow.pjh.is/journal/call-transcription-with-granola*  ·  *Published: 2025-08-13*

---

I use [Granola](https://granola.ai) for call transcription.

## Things I like

1. **You don't have to invite it to meetings.** It just runs on your computer (Mac or Windows) and offers to start automatically when you join a call. No faff, and **no ominous black boxes in the video call**. Just be sure to seek consent.[1](#user-content-fn-1)
1. **Works for any app, including WhatsApp and Signal.**
2. **Summary notes within 3-5 seconds of the call.** And they're easy to edit manually.
3. **Solid iPhone and iPad apps** to record in-person meetings.
4. **One-click share by link, email, copy-paste, Slack etc.** Links can be "visible to anyone with the link" or "visible to anyone on my team".
5. **Easy to customise the summary format** for different call types (e.g. team meeting, user interview).
6. **Easy to enter your own notes.** You can do it during the meeting, and they'll influence the summary. [2](#user-content-fn-2)
1. **Folders:** you can chat with a folder full of notes, and share the folder to your team.
2. **One-click to draft a follow-up email.**
3. **Integrations** so you can automatically send summaries to Notion, Slack, Zapier, etc.
4. **Audio and video is not saved.** I've no reason to store this data after the transcription.
5. **"What did I miss?"**: During the call you can press "What did I miss?" to get a summary of the key points from the last few minutes.

## Things I don't like

1. **Call summaries are good, but not best in class.** I prefer the summaries I get from copy-pasting the raw transcript into ChatGPT GPT-5 Thinking.
2. **Hard to copy the raw transcript:** requires three clicks!
3. **Transcription accuracy is good, but it's not best in class.**
4. **Default privacy setting:** when you install, your meeting notes are "visibile to anyone with the link" by default. You can change this in the settings.

## Limitations

1. **There's no Android app.**
2. **Transcription happens in the cloud**, not locally on your computer. That's fine for most calls, but not for ultra-sensitive stuff.

## The competition

There are loads of options. The strongest competitors include [Google Meet's built-in feature](https://support.google.com/meet/answer/9308681?hl=en), [Circleback](https://circleback.ai), [Fathom](https://fathom.video), [Fireflies](https://fireflies.ai) and [Otter.ai](https://otter.ai).

Some people I know _love_ Granola. I've not heard acquaintances express _love_ for the competitors.

Use cases vary, though. Granola is a non-starter if you need to save the audio or video.

It's hard to track this crowded space: if you try other platforms, I'd love to [hear how you get on](mailto:wow@pjh.is).
