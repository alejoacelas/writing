# Let AI see your calendar, to-do lists and email

*Source: https://wow.pjh.is/journal/let-ai-see-your-calendar-and-email*  ·  *Published: 2025-09-11*

---

If you're using AI to help you [plan your days and weeks](https://wow.pjh.is/journal/week-plans-and-reviews), I recommend giving it access to your calendar, to-do lists, and perhaps your email.

With that, your conversations can include questions like:

- What's new in my email?
- What _must_ I do today?
- What's most important this week?

To do this, configure connectors in [ChatGPT](https://chatgpt.com/#settings/Connectors) and [Claude](https://claude.ai/settings/connectors). Both support Gmail, Google Calendar and Asana. Other to-do software is not yet supported.

If you're using Asana, see [here](https://wow.pjh.is/journal/create-asana-tasks-claude).

## Appendix 1\. Cost:benefit of granting access to your email

It's reasonable to worry about giving LLMs access to your email. Personally I think the cost:benefit here acceptable. Here's a BOTEC:

> \*\*Cost: \\$200 / year\*\*   
> 1/50 annual probability of a data breach that I'd pay \\$10,000\[^1\] to avoid.
> 
> **Benefit: $5,000 / year**   
> $100 per week = $5,000 / year.

**Sensitivity:** if the data breach risk were 1/10, it'd remain mathematically ok, but deontic considerations might rule it out.

**Bottom line:** Try it. Set a reminder to consider disabling it in a month from now, if it turns out you rarely use it.
