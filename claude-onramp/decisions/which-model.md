# Which model?

**Use the best one. Always. On anything you care about.**

Models come in tiers — a fast one, a standard one, and a most-capable one
(Anthropic's are Haiku, Sonnet, Opus). It's tempting to reach for a cheaper or
faster model to save time or money. Two reasons you'd do that, and why neither
holds:

**"The best model is slower."** True — the strongest model thinks longer. But
you don't beat that by downgrading the model; you beat it by not waiting. Start
the task, switch to another window, and do the next thing. Speed comes from
running several at once, not from a weaker mind on the one that matters.

**"The best model costs more."** It does, and it's still the cheapest thing in
the exchange. The task you're delegating is worth far more to you than the few
dollars of difference — that's *why* you're delegating it. If you run out of
credits, buy more; you'll come out ahead. You may not believe this yet. You
will after a week.

So there's no real decision here. Pick the top model, leave it there, and spend
your attention on the work instead of the dial.

*The exception that proves the rule: for a genuinely trivial, high-volume,
throwaway task where you'll glance at the output and move on, a fast model is
fine. When it matters, top model.*
