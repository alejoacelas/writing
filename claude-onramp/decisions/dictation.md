# Talking to Claude instead of typing

**Recommendation: install Wispr Flow and dictate almost everything you say to
Claude.**

The single habit that most improves what you get back is talking instead of
typing. Not because it's faster to produce words — because it changes *which*
words you produce. When you type, you tidy up: you trim the context, drop the
caveats, and hand over a clean-looking request that's missing half of what's in
your head. When you talk, you ramble — and the ramble is the good part. The
aside about why you care, the two approaches you're torn between, the thing
you're unsure about: that's exactly the context that makes Claude's answer fit.

So say the messy version out loud. State the problem, say what you want, name
what you're unsure about, and don't clean it up.

**The tool: [Wispr Flow](https://wisprflow.ai).** It transcribes your speech
into whatever text field you're in, anywhere on your computer, faster and more
accurately than the built-in dictation on Mac or Windows, and it handles the
rambling gracefully. There are alternatives (macOS/Windows built-in dictation,
FluidVoice, others); Wispr is the one worth paying for. Install it, set a
push-to-talk key, and use it for every message longer than a sentence.
